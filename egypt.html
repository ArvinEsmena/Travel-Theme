Welcome to Egypt (أهلاً بكم في مصر)

Where ancient wonders meet timeless beauty

Egypt offers an unparalleled journey through time, where colossal temples and mysterious pyramids stand as testaments to one of history's greatest civilizations. From cruising the legendary Nile River to exploring vibrant bazaars filled with spices and treasures, Egypt captivates visitors with its rich heritage, warm hospitality, and breathtaking landscapes. Experience the magic of the desert, the mystery of the Pharaohs, and the vibrant culture that has flourished along the Nile for thousands of years.

"مصر أم الدنيا" - Egypt is the mother of the world

(Ancient Egyptian proverb)

Egypt's Wonders (عجائب مصر)

Discover the must-see destinations across Egypt

Great Pyramids (أهرامات الجيزة)

The last surviving wonder of the ancient world, the Pyramids of Giza have stood for over 4,500 years. This complex includes the Great Pyramid of Khufu, the Pyramid of Khafre, the Pyramid of Menkaure, and the Great Sphinx. These architectural marvels continue to awe visitors with their scale and precision.

Valley of the Kings (وادي الملوك)

The burial place of Egypt's New Kingdom pharaohs, the Valley of the Kings contains 63 magnificent royal tombs, including that of the famous Tutankhamun. Located on the west bank of the Nile near Luxor, this UNESCO World Heritage site features elaborately decorated tombs carved deep into the desert rock.

Abu Simbel (أبو سمبل)

The massive rock-cut temples of Abu Simbel, built by Ramesses II in the 13th century BCE, stand as a monumental feat of ancient engineering. The complex was miraculously relocated in the 1960s to save it from rising waters caused by the Aswan High Dam. The temples are famous for their colossal statues and precise alignment with the sun.

Taste of Egypt (طعم مصر)

Discover Egypt's rich culinary traditions

Koshari

Egypt's national dish - a delicious mix of rice, lentils, chickpeas, and pasta, topped with spicy tomato sauce and crispy fried onions.

Ful Medames

A traditional breakfast dish made from fava beans cooked with oil, garlic, and lemon juice, typically served with pita bread.

Egyptian Tea

Strong black tea often served with fresh mint leaves and plenty of sugar, a staple of Egyptian hospitality.

Arabic Phrases (عبارات عربية)

Essential phrases for your Egyptian adventure

Hello أهلاً (Ahlan)

Common greeting meaning "welcome".

Thank you شكراً (Shukran)

Essential polite phrase for any situation.

Please من فضلك (Min fadlak)

Used when making a request (to a male).

How much? بكام؟ (Bikam?)

Useful when shopping in markets.

Goodbye مع السلامة (Ma'assalama)

Common way to say goodbye, meaning "with safety".

Cultural Tips (نصائح ثقافية)

Understanding Egyptian customs and etiquette

Dining Etiquette

Egyptians typically eat with their right hand, as the left is considered unclean. Always accept offers of tea or coffee as it's a sign of hospitality. When dining with Egyptians, expect to be offered second and third helpings - it's polite to accept at least a little more.

Dress Code

Egypt is a predominantly Muslim country, so it's respectful to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Women should cover their shoulders and knees, and both men and women should avoid revealing clothing. In tourist areas, dress codes are more relaxed but still appreciated.

Greetings

Egyptians are generally warm and friendly. A handshake is the common greeting, but close friends and family may exchange kisses on the cheek. Always use your right hand for eating and greeting. It's common to see same-gender friends holding hands or walking arm-in-arm as a sign of friendship.
