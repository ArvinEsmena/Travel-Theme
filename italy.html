Welcome to Italy

Where history, art, and cuisine create la dolce vita

Italy offers an unforgettable journey through time, from the ancient Roman Empire to the Renaissance and beyond. From the romantic canals of Venice to the rolling hills of Tuscany, Italy captivates visitors with its stunning landscapes, world-renowned art, and the warm hospitality of its people. Indulge in authentic pasta and pizza, sip wine in centuries-old vineyards, and immerse yourself in a culture that celebrates the beautiful things in life.

Italy's Wonders

Discover the must-see destinations across Italy

Colosseum

The iconic symbol of Imperial Rome, this ancient amphitheater is the largest ever built. Completed in 80 AD, it could hold between 50,000 and 80,000 spectators who watched gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, and dramatic performances. Today, it stands as a magnificent ruin and testament to Roman engineering.

Venice Canals

Built on 118 small islands separated by canals and linked by over 400 bridges, Venice is a city unlike any other. The Grand Canal snakes through the city, past Renaissance palaces and under iconic bridges. A gondola ride through these waterways offers a unique perspective of this magical city that seems to float on water.

Florence Duomo

The Cattedrale di Santa Maria del Fiore, known simply as the Duomo, is the iconic landmark of Florence. Its magnificent dome, engineered by Filippo Brunelleschi, was the largest in the world when completed in 1436. The exterior is faced with polychrome marble panels in shades of green and pink, bordered by white, creating a stunning visual effect.

Cultural Tips

Understanding Italian customs and etiquette

Dining Etiquette

Italians take their food seriously. Never ask for cheese to put on seafood pasta â€“ it's considered a culinary crime. Cappuccino is only drunk in the morning, and dinner is typically later, around 8-9 PM. Always say "buon appetito" before eating.

Dress Code

Italians are known for their style. When visiting churches or religious sites, make sure to cover your shoulders and knees. Avoid wearing shorts or sleeveless tops in these sacred spaces. Bring a scarf to cover up if needed.

Greetings

Italians are generally warm and expressive. A light kiss on both cheeks is common between friends and acquaintances. Handshakes are appropriate in formal situations. Always greet shopkeepers when entering small stores with a "buongiorno" or "buonasera".
