Welcome to Japan (日本へようこそ)

Where ancient traditions meet modern innovation

Japan offers a unique travel experience where ancient temples stand beside neon skyscrapers. From the serene beauty of Mount Fuji to the bustling streets of Tokyo, Japan captivates visitors with its perfect harmony of tradition and innovation, exquisite cuisine, and unparalleled natural beauty. Experience the tranquility of Zen gardens, the excitement of high-speed bullet trains, and the warmth of omotenashi (Japanese hospitality).

"七転び八起き" - Japanese Proverb

("Fall seven times, stand up eight")

Japan's Wonders (日本の驚異)

Discover the must-see destinations across Japan

Mount Fuji (富士山)

Japan's tallest peak and an active volcano, Mount Fuji is one of the country's most iconic symbols. Its nearly perfect symmetrical cone has inspired artists and pilgrims for centuries. Designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2013, it's considered one of Japan's 3 Sacred Mountains.

Fushimi Inari Shrine (伏見稲荷大社)

Famous for its thousands of vibrant red torii gates that wind through the forests of Kyoto's sacred Mount Inari. This Shinto shrine is dedicated to the god of rice and prosperity. The hike to the summit takes about 2-3 hours and passes through thousands of torii gates donated by businesses and individuals.

Kinkaku-ji (金閣寺)

Also known as the Golden Pavilion, this Zen Buddhist temple in Kyoto is one of Japan's most famous buildings. The top two floors are completely covered in gold leaf, reflecting beautifully in the surrounding pond. Originally built in 1397 as a retirement villa for shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu, it was converted into a temple after his death.

Taste of Japan (日本の味)

Discover Japan's world-renowned culinary traditions

Sushi & Sashimi

Fresh, expertly prepared raw fish served over vinegared rice (sushi) or alone (sashimi). A true art form in Japanese cuisine.

Ramen

Hearty noodle soup with various broths (soy, miso, pork) topped with sliced pork, nori, menma, and green onions.

Matcha & Wagashi

Traditional Japanese green tea served with delicate, artistic sweets that change with the seasons.

Cultural Tips (文化のヒント)

Understanding Japanese customs and etiquette

Bowing

Bowing is an important custom in Japan used to show respect, gratitude, or apology. The depth and duration of the bow depends on the situation and relationship between people.

Removing Shoes

It's customary to remove your shoes when entering homes, temples, traditional inns (ryokan), and even some restaurants. Look for slippers provided by the establishment.

Dining Etiquette

When eating with chopsticks, never stick them upright in rice (associated with funerals). It's polite to say "itadakimasu" before eating and "gochisosama deshita" after finishing.
