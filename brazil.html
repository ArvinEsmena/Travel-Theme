Welcome to Brazil

Where vibrant culture meets natural wonders

Brazil offers an incredible diversity of experiences from the Amazon rainforest to pristine beaches, from the rhythm of samba to the excitement of football. As the fifth largest country in the world, Brazil boasts an incredible array of natural wonders, vibrant cities, and a rich cultural heritage that blends indigenous, African, and European influences. Experience the warmth of Brazilian hospitality, the flavors of its diverse cuisine, and the joy of its celebrations.

Brazil's Wonders

Christ the Redeemer

Standing atop Corcovado Mountain in Rio de Janeiro, this iconic statue of Jesus Christ is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. The 98-foot-tall art deco statue offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city and has become a cultural symbol of Brazil worldwide.

Iguazu Falls

One of the world's most spectacular waterfall systems, Iguazu Falls straddles the border between Brazil and Argentina. With 275 individual drops spread in a horseshoe shape along nearly 2 miles, the falls are taller than Niagara and wider than Victoria Falls.

Cultural Tips

Understanding Brazilian customs and etiquette

Greetings

Brazilians are warm and affectionate people. Greetings often involve hugs and kisses on the cheek, even between people who have just met. A firm handshake with eye contact is also common in business settings.

Dining Customs

Brazilian meals are often social events. It's polite to keep your hands visible above the table during meals. When eating at someone's home, always compliment the cook and try a little of everything offered.
